High-Fat Nutrition & Feminine Wellness

How whole foods, healthy fats, and low-carb living support your body's natural radiance

For years, I believed that more discipline and restriction was the answer. But what truly transformed my relationship with food—and my body—was learning to nourish rather than deprive.

A high-fat, low-carb, whole foods approach isn't just another diet. It's a way of eating that supports your body's natural rhythms, stabilizes your energy, and creates the foundation for true vitality. When your blood sugar is stable and your hormones are balanced, you naturally feel calmer, more present, and deeply grounded in your body.

This isn't about perfection or rigid rules. It's about discovering what makes your body feel truly alive—energized without being wired, satisfied without being heavy, clear without being foggy. It's about eating in a way that supports you in showing up as your most radiant, magnetic self.

The Nutrition-Nervous System Connection

What you eat directly affects how you feel—not just physically, but emotionally and energetically.

Blood Sugar & Your Nervous System

When your blood sugar spikes and crashes throughout the day, your body interprets this as stress. Your sympathetic nervous system (fight-or-flight) activates, releasing cortisol and adrenaline. You feel anxious, irritable, and on edge.

A high-fat, low-carb approach keeps your blood sugar stable, which allows your parasympathetic nervous system (rest-and-digest) to remain active. This is where calm lives. Where presence lives. Where feminine energy flows.

What this feels like: You wake up energized without needing coffee immediately. You move through your day without the 3pm crash. You feel steady, grounded, and capable of being present with whatever arises.

Nourishment as Self-Care

The way you feed yourself is an act of love. When you choose whole, nutrient-dense foods—pasture-raised meats, wild-caught fish, organic vegetables, healthy fats like avocado, olive oil, and grass-fed butter—you're telling your body: you matter. You deserve to feel good.

This isn't about restriction or discipline. It's about abundance. It's about discovering that the foods that make you feel incredible also happen to be the most nourishing.

What this looks like: A beautiful salmon fillet with roasted vegetables and avocado. Eggs cooked in butter with fresh herbs. A rich cup of bone broth. Food that satisfies deeply, that feels like medicine, that makes your body sing.

From Doing to Being

When you're running on sugar and carbs, you need constant input—food, caffeine, stimulation. You're always in doing mode, always seeking the next hit of energy or dopamine.

When you're fat-adapted, your energy is steady and enduring. You don't need to constantly refuel. This creates space—space to pause, to breathe, to simply be. You're not controlled by your next meal or snack. You're free to be present.

My experience: I used to need snacks every 2-3 hours and would get "hangry" if I didn't eat. Now, I can go hours without thinking about food. My energy is stable. My mood is stable. And that stability creates space for presence, connection, and joy.

The Research Behind It

Emerging research shows that a high-fat, low-carb, ketogenic approach can support hormone balance (particularly for PCOS and irregular cycles), improve insulin sensitivity, reduce inflammation, support brain health, and even improve endurance performance.

For women specifically, this way of eating can support estrogen metabolism, reduce period symptoms, improve fertility markers, and create the stable foundation needed for natural hormone balance.

Worth noting: Every body is different. Some women thrive on strict keto, others feel best with slightly more carbs (especially around their cycle). This is about discovering what makes YOUR body feel incredible, not following someone else's rules.

Supplements That Support My Journey

What works for my body—not medical advice, just personal experience.

Important note: I'm not a doctor, and this isn't medical advice. These are supplements I personally use and find helpful in supporting my energy, vitality, and well-being. Everyone's body is different—what works for me might not work for you. Always consult with a healthcare professional before adding new supplements to your routine, especially if you have existing health conditions or take medications.

Vitamin D3 with K2

Most of us are deficient in vitamin D—especially if we live in northern climates, spend most of our time indoors, or have darker skin. Vitamin D isn't just about bone health; it's crucial for immune function, mood regulation, hormone balance, and even inflammation.

I take D3 combined with K2 because they work synergistically. D3 helps your body absorb calcium, and K2 directs that calcium to your bones and teeth (where you want it) instead of your arteries (where you don't). Together, they support bone health, cardiovascular health, and overall vitality.

How It Supports You:
  • Mood & Mental Health: D3 supports serotonin production, helping with seasonal mood changes and overall emotional balance
  • Immune Function: Essential for a strong, resilient immune system
  • Bone Health: Works with K2 to build and maintain strong bones
  • Hormone Balance: Supports healthy hormone production, particularly important for feminine wellness
  • Energy & Vitality: Low D3 is linked to fatigue—adequate levels support sustained energy

My experience: Since supplementing with D3+K2, I notice a significant difference in my energy levels, especially during winter months. My mood feels more stable, and I recover better from workouts. I take 5,000 IU daily, but optimal dosing varies—some people need more, some less.

Magnesium Bis-glycinate

Magnesium is involved in over 300 biochemical reactions in your body—from energy production to nervous system regulation to muscle relaxation. Most of us are deficient, and it shows up as anxiety, poor sleep, muscle cramps, fatigue, and hormonal imbalances.

I specifically take magnesium bis-glycinate (also called magnesium glycinate) because it's the most bioavailable and gentle form. Unlike other forms (like magnesium oxide), bis-glycinate is easily absorbed and doesn't cause digestive upset. It's bonded to the amino acid glycine, which has its own calming properties.

How It Supports You:
  • Nervous System Regulation: Calms the nervous system, reduces anxiety, and supports a parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) state
  • Sleep Quality: Promotes deep, restorative sleep by relaxing muscles and calming the mind
  • Muscle Recovery: Prevents cramps, supports muscle relaxation, and aids recovery after exercise
  • Hormone Balance: Supports healthy progesterone production and reduces PMS symptoms
  • Energy Production: Essential for converting food into usable energy (ATP)
  • Blood Sugar Regulation: Improves insulin sensitivity and supports stable blood sugar

My experience: Magnesium has been transformative for my sleep and nervous system. I take 300-400mg before bed, and I notice I fall asleep faster, sleep deeper, and wake up more rested. It also helps with muscle soreness after long runs or bike rides. On days I forget to take it, I can feel the difference—my sleep is lighter and my body feels more tense.

Apple Cider Vinegar with the Mother

Apple cider vinegar (ACV) with "the mother" is raw, unfiltered, unpasteurized vinegar that contains strands of beneficial enzymes, proteins, and friendly bacteria. It's an ancient tonic that's been used for centuries to support vitality, digestion, and overall life force.

Unlike regular vinegar, ACV with the mother is alive—full of probiotics and enzymes that support your gut health, blood sugar regulation, and metabolism. It's one of those simple, powerful remedies that feels like it awakens something in your body. A daily dose of ACV can help you feel clearer, lighter, and more energized.

How It Supports You:
  • Digestion & Gut Health: Stimulates digestive enzymes and stomach acid production, supporting nutrient absorption and reducing bloating
  • Blood Sugar Balance: Helps moderate blood sugar spikes after meals, supporting stable energy and reducing cravings
  • Metabolism & Detoxification: Supports liver function and gentle detoxification, helping your body process and eliminate waste
  • Energy & Vitality: Many people report feeling more energized and clear-headed after incorporating ACV into their routine
  • Skin Health: Can improve skin clarity from the inside out by supporting gut health and detoxification pathways
  • Immune Support: The probiotics and enzymes in the mother support a healthy gut microbiome, which is foundational for immune health
How to Use It:
  1. Always dilute: Mix 1-2 tablespoons of raw ACV with 8-12oz of water. Never drink it straight—undiluted ACV can damage tooth enamel and irritate your throat
  2. Best time: First thing in the morning on an empty stomach, or 15-20 minutes before meals to support digestion and blood sugar balance
  3. Optional additions: Add a squeeze of lemon, a pinch of cinnamon, or a drizzle of raw honey to make it more palatable
  4. Use a straw: Sip through a straw to minimize contact with your teeth, then rinse your mouth with plain water afterward
  5. Start slow: If you're new to ACV, start with 1 teaspoon and gradually increase to 1-2 tablespoons as your body adjusts
  6. Choose quality: Look for raw, unfiltered, organic ACV with the mother (brands like Bragg's are widely available)

My experience: I drink ACV most mornings—1 tablespoon in a glass of water with lemon juice and a pinch of sea salt. It's become a grounding ritual that signals to my body: we're starting the day with intention. I notice it helps with digestion, especially if I'm eating heavier meals or more carbs than usual. It gives me a subtle energy lift without the jitters of caffeine, and over time, I've noticed my skin looks clearer and my digestion feels smoother. It's one of those simple practices that makes a noticeable difference when I'm consistent with it.

Supplements are meant to supplement, not replace, a nutrient-dense diet. Food first, always. But when used wisely, quality supplements can fill in the gaps and support your body's natural capacity for vitality, balance, and radiance.

Hydration & Electrolytes

Proper hydration is the foundation of energy, vitality, and clear thinking. But it's not just about water—it's about balance.

You've probably heard "drink more water" a thousand times. But here's what most people don't realize: water alone isn't enough. If you're drinking plain water all day without adequate electrolytes, you're flushing minerals out of your body faster than you can replace them. The result? Fatigue, brain fog, headaches, muscle cramps, and that constant feeling of being thirsty no matter how much you drink.

True hydration requires electrolytes—minerals that regulate fluid balance, nerve function, muscle contraction, and energy production. When your electrolytes are balanced, your cells can actually absorb and use the water you're drinking. You feel energized, clear-headed, and grounded in your body.

This is especially important if you're eating low-carb or keto, exercising regularly, or living in a hot climate. When you reduce carbs, your body releases water and electrolytes more rapidly. When you sweat, you lose electrolytes. If you're not intentionally replacing them, you'll feel depleted—even if you're drinking tons of water.

The Three Essential Electrolytes

Sodium

Role: Regulates fluid balance, supports nerve transmission, maintains blood pressure, and helps cells absorb nutrients and water

Signs you need more: Headaches, fatigue, dizziness, muscle cramps, brain fog, low energy

We've been told to fear salt, but adequate sodium is essential—especially on low-carb or if you're active

Potassium

Role: Supports heart function, muscle contraction, nerve signaling, and cellular hydration. Balances sodium and regulates blood pressure

Signs you need more: Muscle weakness, cramps, heart palpitations, fatigue, constipation

Potassium is abundant in whole foods like avocados, leafy greens, and salmon—prioritize food sources first

Magnesium

Role: Involved in 300+ reactions—energy production, muscle relaxation, nervous system regulation, sleep, and more

Signs you need more: Anxiety, insomnia, muscle cramps, twitching eyelid, fatigue, irritability

Most people are deficient in magnesium—it's depleted by stress, exercise, and modern diets

Why Electrolyte Balance Matters for Feminine Health

Electrolyte balance isn't just about hydration—it's deeply connected to feminine wellness. When your electrolytes are balanced, your hormones function better, your energy is stable, your nervous system is calm, and your body can operate at its best.

How Electrolytes Support You:
  • Hormone Production: Adequate sodium and potassium are essential for adrenal health, which directly impacts hormone balance
  • Energy & Vitality: Electrolytes enable your cells to produce ATP (energy), preventing that constant fatigue and brain fog
  • Nervous System Regulation: Magnesium especially helps shift you from fight-or-flight into rest-and-digest, supporting calm and presence
  • Menstrual Health: Proper hydration and electrolyte balance can reduce bloating, cramps, and headaches during your cycle
  • Exercise Recovery: Electrolytes prevent cramps, support muscle function, and help you recover faster from workouts
  • Mental Clarity: Your brain is 75% water—adequate hydration with electrolytes means sharper thinking and better focus

Practical Tips for Staying Hydrated

It's not complicated, but it does require intention. Here's how to ensure you're truly hydrated—not just drinking water, but nourishing your cells.

Daily Hydration Practices:
  1. Start your day with salt water: First thing in the morning, drink 16oz of water with a pinch of high-quality sea salt (like Redmond Real Salt or Himalayan pink salt). This kickstarts hydration and supports your adrenals.
  2. Add electrolytes to your water: Use a quality electrolyte powder (look for ones without sugar or artificial ingredients) or make your own with sea salt, lemon juice, and a magnesium supplement.
  3. Drink to thirst: You don't need to force-drink gallons of water. Listen to your body. If your urine is pale yellow, you're hydrated. If it's clear, you might be over-hydrating and flushing out minerals.
  4. Salt your food generously: Don't be afraid of salt. Use high-quality sea salt on your meals. Your body will tell you if you need more—trust your cravings.
  5. Eat water-rich, mineral-dense foods: Leafy greens, cucumber, celery, bone broth, avocados, and wild-caught fish all provide hydration and electrolytes.
  6. Before/during/after exercise: Drink water with electrolytes, especially if you're sweating. A pinch of salt in your water bottle can prevent cramps and fatigue.
  7. Support magnesium levels: Since it's hard to get enough through food alone, consider supplementing with magnesium bis-glycinate (see above).

My routine: I start every morning with 16oz of water mixed with a pinch of sea salt and lemon juice. Throughout the day, I drink water with added electrolytes (especially before long runs or bike rides). I salt my food generously and eat lots of leafy greens and avocados. I take magnesium at night. This simple routine has completely transformed my energy, mental clarity, and how I feel in my body.

Hydration is one of the simplest, most powerful things you can do for your body. When you're properly hydrated with balanced electrolytes, you feel it—your energy is steady, your mind is clear, your body feels alive. This is foundational self-care.

Remember: this isn't about perfection. It's not about never having a slice of sourdough or a piece of dark chocolate. It's about discovering a way of eating that makes you feel alive, energized, and deeply connected to your body. It's about nourishing yourself in a way that supports your nervous system, your hormones, and your capacity to be present, joyful, and radiant.
